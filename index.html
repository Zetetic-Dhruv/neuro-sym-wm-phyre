Does the world model track physics?

A clean verification of the TOKW v0.4.0 world model on PHYRE. The same VLM-trained model from the original run is taken unchanged; its predictions (seeded from the VLM-observed first frame, identical to that run) are scored against two referees — the Qwen VLM labels it was trained to mimic, and PHYRE's exact physics computed from featurized_objects. Ground truth is only the answer key: it never enters training or the seed.

Read this honestly. Held fixed and re-graded against real physics, the model agrees with the Qwen oracle it was trained on (~0.97) far more than with the world (~0.72). At the moments that matter it predicts physical transitions correctly only ~49% of the time and spuriously flips propositions at 27% of stable frames — it has learned to reproduce the VLM's labelling, not the environment's dynamics.

The model is a faithful mirror of the labeller. Its physics numbers (0.721 / 0.490) are almost identical to the labeller's (0.720 / 0.489): the model inherits the labeller's ~28% physical error and its at-chance transitions exactly — neither correcting nor compounding them. The neuro-symbolic model works as designed (it propagates its symbol source); its physical fidelity is simply capped by the oracle it imitates, and that oracle is a mediocre physics labeller.

A, B, C, D, F are statistically indistinguishable

Inside a band of 0.004 on per-frame and 0.006 on transition accuracy. Repeating the first observed VLM frame indefinitely (B) matches the full TOKW v0.4.0 rollout (F) on every column. The two truth-only retrains (C, D) land on the same point. On this benchmark the latent encoder z, the action channel a, and the truth-dynamics transformer add no measurable physical fidelity above persistence of v0.

E is worse, not equal

The Kripke Markov-chain ablation drops per-frame by 0.036 relative to F and raises spurious-flip from 0.270 to 0.307. Discretising the continuous truth into a finite state set and propagating with a learned transition matrix actively destroys information rather than matching the trivial baseline. It is the one configuration that moves the needle, and it moves it the wrong way.

What the 0.72 number actually is

The constant all-zeros predictor scores 0.723 on this schema (the mean per-AP zero rate is 0.7229). Per-AP majority scores 0.833, persist-frame-0 scores 0.870. Every model variant in the panel is at or below the trivial-zero floor. The 0.72 band is the marginal label rate of the physics-derived verify schema, not a "robust floor every method reaches" — it is the floor below which methods cannot get worse on a sparse-label schema without actively inserting positives.

Metric caveats from the audit.

frame_change_f1 omitted. The earlier draft reported a column that tied at 0.3984 across all six configs. That is a degenerate property of the metric definition: it compares each prediction at t+1 to physics-truth at t, saturating fr_pred to True on essentially every valid frame, forcing recall=1 and F1 = 2p/(1+p) at the physics frame-change rate p=0.249. It carries no information about any ablation and is dropped rather than footnoted.

C and D retrain logs are silent. The retrain function emits no per-epoch loss or early-stop notice. C and D landing exactly on the B persistence-of-v0 line is the expected behaviour of a small transformer that learned the trivial copy-prev-v solution given a 96% frame-to-frame persistence rate, and it is consistent with the rest of the panel — but until a relogged rerun lands, treat C and D as consistent with the headline rather than independent confirmation of it.

Per-frame accuracy is not a ranking metric on this schema. With a 72.3% zero rate it cannot separate any of A, B, C, D, F from each other or from all-zeros. Transition accuracy (~0.49, at chance for a 3.84% transition fraction) is the honest column.

Implication: whatever the architecture is for, it is not narrowing the gap to PHYRE physics on this schema. That gap is set by the VLM labeller and no component measured here closes it.
